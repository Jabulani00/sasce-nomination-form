Section 2 of 9

Criteria for Election to the SASCE Office Bearers

When considering nominating members to serve on the SASCE Board, the following governance standards supported by King IV should be considered:

  • Any member in good standing is eligible to nominate or be nominated to serve as a SASCE Elected Official
  • An appropriate mix of knowledge, skills, and experience is required of each nominee, including requisite business acumen and industry experience necessary to govern SASCE.
  • The need for elected members who qualify to lead other functional sub-committees.
  • Reasonable capacity and official support ensuring member's availability for SASCE duty and quorated meetings to drive effectiveness of the Board.
  • The need to comply with legislative and statutory requirements relating to diversity in the composition of the SASCE Board, in terms of gender, race, and skills.
  • The need to have a succession plan in place to ensure continuity and sustainability of SASCE operations.
  • The need for candidates that have a broad and extensive network, with capacity to organise and mobilise resources for the benefit of SASCE.
