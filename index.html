Section 2 of 7

Criteria for Election to the SASCE Office Bearers

When considering nominating members to serve on the SASCE Board, the following governance standards supported by King IV should be considered:

  1. Any member in good standing is eligible to nominate or be nominated to serve as a SASCE Elected Official
  2. An appropriate mix of knowledge, skills, and experience is required of each nominee, including requisite business acumen and industry experience necessary to govern SASCE.
  3. The need for elected members who qualify to lead other functional sub-committees.
  4. Reasonable capacity and official support ensuring member's availability for SASCE duty and quorated meetings to drive effectiveness of the Board.
  5. The need to comply with legislative and statutory requirements relating to diversity in the composition of the SASCE Board, in terms of gender, race, and skills.
  6. The need to have a succession plan in place to ensure continuity and sustainability of SASCE operations.
  7. The need for candidates that have a broad and extensive network, with capacity to organise and mobilise resources for the benefit of SASCE.
